How to use it

There are four conversion tables: flow/volumes and prices for the most common gases (oxygen, nitrogen, argon, carbon dioxide, hydrogen and helium); temperatures; and pressures.
Just click on a cell of one of the conversion tables, enter a new value, validate (by pressing the 'enter' key) and all the other related cells of the table will be updated automatically.
A click on the image located in the left part of the header (the trusty HAL 9000) will bring you back to the home panel.

Main assumptions:

  • Nm3 (normal cubic meter) gas measured at 1 atmosphere (1,013.25 mbar, 29.92inHg) and 0°C (32°F)
  • Sm3 (standard cubic meter) gas measured at 1 atmosphere and 15°C (59°F)
  • Scf (standard cubic foot) gas measured at 1 atmosphere and 70°F (21.1°C)
  • Liquid density measured at 1 atmosphere and boiling temperature, except for CO2 measured at triple point (-56.6°C, 5.18 bar)
  • Reference: Air Liquide Gas Encyclopedia
